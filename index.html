WORK EXPERIENCE

OHIO STATE DEPARTMENT OF ASTRONOMY

Research Assistant, May 2017 - Present

I started working under Dr. Annika Peter as a part of the summer undergraduate research program at the department of astronomy at OSU, which is funded by NASA and the NSF. The project is aimed towards finding a more direct method to estimate the mass of weak dark matter halos. This is done by using various data analysis techniques to closely study these satellite galaxies, and find a way to associate their kinematics, such as relative velocity, to the halo's mass.

GIRLS WHO CODE

Instructor, June 2016 - Aug 2016

The Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program is a 7 week coding camp that focuses on narrowing the gender gap by teaching high school girls more about technology and encouraging them to pursue STEM fields for higher studies. My role as a part of an instructional team of 2 other amazing computer scientists was to engage with the girls and make the lecture material interesting, easy to understand, and accessible. We were also responsible for arranging field trips and guest speakers, and finally helping the girls create a personal project which best highlighted their passions and skill set.
